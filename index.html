SMART Goals

SMART is a framework for setting clear, trackable, and achievable goals. Each letter stands for one quality your goal should have.

SSpecificClearly define what you want to accomplish. Avoid vague statements.
MMeasurableInclude numbers or criteria so you can track your progress.
AAchievableMake sure the goal is realistic given your resources and time.
RRelevantThe goal should matter to you and align with your broader objectives.
TTime-BoundSet a deadline or timeframe to create urgency and focus.

Examples

Good: Complete 3 chapters of Linear Algebra by March 30 with 80%+ quiz score.

Bad: Study more math.

Good: Read 20 pages of English literature every day for 2 weeks.

Bad: Read more books.
